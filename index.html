Chobe National Park

Chobe National Park, in northern Botswana, has one of the largest concentrations of game in Africa. The Serondela area features lush floodplains and dense woodland, serving as a major watering spot for large breeding herds of elephants, giraffes, sable and cape buffalo. The flood plains are the only place in Botswana where the puku antelope can be seen.

Savuti

The Savuti Marsh area constitutes the western stretch of Chobe park. Fed by the erratic Savuti Channel, this region features extensive savannahs and rolling grasslands. Savuti is renowned for its annual migration of zebras and predators, with excellent opportunities to view lions, hyenas, and wildlife interactions.

Moremi Game Reserve

Moremi Game Reserve covers much of the eastern side of the Okavango Delta, combining permanent water with drier areas. Home to nearly 500 species of birds and vast wildlife including the rare African wild dog and leopard. The reserve offers excellent savannah game viewing and lagoon birdwatching.

Okavango Delta

The Okavango Delta is home to a wide variety of wildlife including African bush elephant, hippos, sitatunga, Nile crocodile, and Southwest African lion. With over 400 bird species and an estimated 200,000 large mammals, it's one of Africa's premier wildlife destinations with unique mokoro (canoe) experiences.
